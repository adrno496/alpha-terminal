Why Alpha wins on privacy

Your watchlists, portfolios, search history: on Koyfin everything sits on their AWS servers. Legal, classic — but it means a Koyfin employee (or a breach) could see you've been accumulating Hermès for 6 months, or that 47% of your wealth is in NVIDIA. For many that's fine. For anyone running a small RIA, a family office, or managing client money: it's a dealbreaker.

Alpha runs 100% client-side. Your portfolio, notes and RAG documents stay in your browser's IndexedDB. No Alpha server ever sees this data. When you run an AI analysis, your request goes from your browser straight to OpenAI/Anthropic via your API key (BYOK). Alpha is never the middleman. It's technically verifiable: open DevTools Network tab, you'll never see a call to an Alpha server for your data.
